Motivation

Why was the dataset created?

  • The Human Phenotype Ontology was created to provide a standardized vocabulary for the phenotypic features encountered in human diseases. It enables computational analysis of disease manifestations, facilitates data integration across medical and research databases, and supports clinical diagnosis and genetic research by providing a common language for describing patient phenotypes.

Human Phenotype Ontology (HPO)
The Human Phenotype Ontology is a standardized, structured, and controlled vocabulary for describing phenotypic features encountered in human hereditary and other diseases. It provides a comprehensive set of terms for clinical features observed in human patients, enabling structured annotation of phenotypic abnormalities in medical and research contexts. The ontology enables computational analysis of disease manifestations and facilitates integration of phenotype data across different sources.
