https://doi.org/10.18130/V3/B35XWX
CM4AI
Cell Maps for Artificial Intelligence (CM4AI) Data Generation Project
The Cell Maps for Artificial Intelligence (CM4AI) is a NIH-funded Bridge to Artificial Intelligence (Bridge2AI) Data Generation Project in the area of Functional Genomics. CM4AI's objective is to deliver machine-readable hierarchical maps of cell architecture as AI-Ready data produced from multimodal interrogation of 100 chromatin modifiers and 100 metabolic enzymes involved in cancer, neuropsychiatric, and cardiac disorders in disease-relevant cell lines under perturbed and unperturbed conditions. The dataset uses state-of-the-art mass spectrometry based proteomics (AP-MS, SEC-MS), spatial proteomics via immunofluorescence cell imaging, and genetic perturbations using CRISPR/Cas9. Cell lines under investigation include treated and untreated MDA-MB-468 breast cancer cell lines (paclitaxel and vorinostat), and differentiated neurons and cardiomyocytes generated from NIH-iPCS-1 KOLF2.1J induced pluripotent stem cells (IPSCs). CM4AI's software pipeline and AI-readiness framework provide important enabling capabilities for this work, packaging data with provenance graphs and rich metadata in RO-Crate format using the FAIRSCAPE framework.
